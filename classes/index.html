Classes for online Workshop
July 10-12, 2020

Parents please note:

  • Children must be supervised by a parent or guardian. Parent participation in instrumental classes will ensure the best Suzuki experience for all.
  • Registered participants will receive their individual schedules by email.

Included in the weekend Workshop:

Masterclass

All participating students in Book 1 and up and ages 5+ have an individualized masterclass once per day on Saturday and Sunday, working with the same faculty each day for consistency. Please prepare a polished Suzuki piece, chosen by the student and their home teacher. These classes are limited to maximum four students and will be scheduled throughout the weekend. Families may indicate a time preference, and may also choose to add an additional masterclass on Friday if desired. Please indicate these preferences by June 30.

Pre-Twinkle

For violin, viola and cello students ages 4+ who have begun Suzuki study. Students will attend two 50-minute sessions, one per day on Saturday July 11 and Sunday July 12 at 10am Eastern. No instrument yet? No problem! Contact us.

Piano: Let Yourself Go with Improv

Rediscover the fun of music-making, guided by your intuition! Our Piano Improvisation class gives you the opportunity to create music on the spot. Here is your chance to tap into your creativity and artistry for a rewarding musical time. Let yourself go! Students attend two 50-minute sessions, one per day on Saturday and Sunday.

Conducting for Kids

Orchestra this year will be hands-on and ears-on with students taking the podium! Grouped by level. Students attend two 50-minute sessions, one per day on Saturday and Sunday.

Group Classes

These classes are organized by instrument and book level. In a group repertoire class, musical techniques and playing skills will be worked on as the students play their common repertoire. We will also prepare for the final concert.

Folk Song and Movement

John Krumm, multi-instrumentalist and beloved music educator, will lead movement, humorous songs, rounds, partner songs, and folk games, all taught in John’s masterful, magical style. Students in Books 3 and under attend one 50-minute class during the weekend.

Art and Listening

Asynchronous. Acclaimed Suzuki educator Nancy Modell will guide children through a calm, screen-free music and art activity to be enjoyed anywhere during breaks!

Advanced Instrumental Enrichment

New this year for strings and piano students in Books 4-6. Spend time with a clinician on techniques to improve your playing, and expand your repertoire! Students will attend one 50-minute class during the weekend.

Play-In and Concert

The final class period on Sunday afternoon, students and faculty will gather by instrument and present the repertoire covered at the Workshop. Then, all students will join together for a grand finale!

Special Guest Concert

Professional fiddler and former Suzuki kid Haley Richardson will inspire us with a lively after-dinner performance for Generation Z and their families!

Instrument Making Demonstration

Asynchronous. Luthier Cezar Matiescu shows us how he creates a violin and bow step-by-step. You’ll never squish your bridge again!

Parent Talks

Parents, join our faculty for a participatory discussion with our friendly community. We will offer 50-minute sessions on Saturday and Sunday to discuss practice tips, staying motivated through the tween years, and support with issues that may arise at every level of experience. All questions are welcome.
